My Journey Into Tech

My interest in tech probably comes from reading a lot of science fiction as a child, and watching Star Trek: The Next Generation. (I know that my pronunciation of the word "data" comes from TNG). I took Computer Science AP in high school, and enjoyed it, but I didn't really take tech seriously as a career option until I was an adult.

After years of back-breaking and unfulfilling work in retail, construction, etc., I decided go back to school for something that would be more mentally stimulating. My first foray into technology was to get a certificate in CNC operation and programming, which sounded interesting. However, after about a year of doing machine setup and operation with little prospect of landing a programming job in a reasonable amount of time, I went back to school again.

I started with a certificate in Java from my community college, and then completed an intensive boot camp for full-stack web development. Finally, something that really got the brain juices flowing! Since then I've been passing on what I've learned to others through teaching, and working on my own projects, like the ones highlighted here.
